Round 2

I hold a tale that’s twisted tight,
But played too fast, I lose my might.
To hear me true, slow down the run,
To half my pace—now that’s step one.
But even then, I still won’t show,
Unless you make my rivers flow
Backward, where the start is end—
Only then my truth I send.

Now that you've heard my secret tone,
Not as it was, but on your own—
A slower beat, a backward play,
Reveals the path, shows you the way.
This hidden sound, once you unveil,
Will point you toward a game, not stale.
A classic name, a past-time feat,
Its title echoes in the beat.

A soldier’s past begins to fray,
A shadow war where truths decay.
He wore the crown, yet not his own,
A puppet king on a borrowed throne.

Not born but shaped, his fate was steered,
The clone of war the Patriots reared.
Not first, not last, the one between,
A ghostly name, struck from the scene.

Who is he, the man of power and fears,
The vanished President, lost in the years?
